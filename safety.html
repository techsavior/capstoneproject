Spyware is a virus that allows a hacker to obtain information about someones computer activities by secretly stealing data.1 Adware is a software downloaded to a computer that displays or downloads advertisments, without the users knowledge. Trojans are software disguised as another to trick you into installing it.2 Finally ransomware is a software that blocks access to a computer, usually for a large sum of money.3 Scams include Phishing, romance scams, lottery scams, and charity scams.   • How to Spot Scams

    If anything is suspicious do not click it! Well, you may say “How do I know if the link is suspicious?” Well, here are a couple of ways. If there are any misspellings, that is strike one. If the website or the email is trying to sell you something, that is strike two. If they ask for your personal information, that is strike three. At this point, you should be able to see that the content you are viewing is potentially a scam. For phone calls and text messages, generally, if you get a text or call from an unknown person asking you for personal information or trying to sell you something, it is most likely a scam.   • How to Spot Viruses

    Viruses can come in many different forms, and spotting them can become tricky if you are not so tech-savvy. Viruses can hide within your files and sometimes not set off your antivirus software. Here are some ways to spot a virus. If your computer is acting odd in some way such as opening programs that you didn’t open yourself, things like ads or random website pop-ups, slower than usual operation, or your antivirus software has been disabled unexpectedly. If you spot one or more of these symptoms on your computer/phone, delete or quarantine the virus if you can spot it within your files. Another way to remedy your computer is to run your regular antivirus software and have it remove the virus for you. A tool I like to use is something called Virus Total.
