Information Literacy


  • Information literacy is the ability to understand and use the information given effectively. Every bit of information you receive on the internet can be used effectively whether it's to spread awareness of misinformation or to inform a group of people. Spreading misinformation can be very detrimental to the public. You could have plenty of people believing something untruthful because you provided false information. Lots of misinformation was spread online about the COVID-19 pandemic, which sometimes resulted in deaths. It is our job as digital citizens to prevent misunderstandings of information from happening.

    (The IDC, 2018)

  • What do you do with the information you find?

    With any information you find, you must check your sources. Checking your sources ensures that the information you see is factually correct. If the information you provide is factually correct you are less likely to spread misinformation to others. When you check your sources make sure the sources have proper citations, proper credentials such as those created by a professor in the subject or a researcher, and evidence to back statements with things like graphs or statistics. Another way to spot a reliable source is bias, conduct your research on the source well, do they have a history of being biased? Once you have found a reliable source of information you can do what your heart may please. You can create a research paper on the topic, add it to your blog, send it to your friends, and much more.1
