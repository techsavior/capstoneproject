The Digital Divide



  • The Digital Divide is the difference between the amount of people who do have access to technology and the internet and those who do not.

  • Fun Facts:

  • According to the ITU(International Telecommunication Union) “one third of the population is without the internet”.
    (UNICEF, 2020)

  • According to UNICEF “two-thirds of children and young people (people under 25) do not have internet access at home”.
    (UNICEF, 2020)

  • “In 2019, 79 percent of adults worldwide who were full-time employees for employers had access to the internet, while only 20 percent did not.”
    (Petrosyan, 2024)

  • Why is the Digital Divide Important?

    The digital divide is what separates people from being able to receive the information they need. Being disconnected from the internet and technology, in a way, removes you from the world. You miss out on news, you can't communicate in a way that most people do, and you limit yourself to the amount of information you can obtain. Schools are starting to progress to a more technology-based format which in turn requires a form of digital literacy. How would anyone gain digital literacy if they do not have access to technology at home? Closing the gap is the main goal. It can help provide those without jobs with jobs and give people access to education.
